Personal Finance

AC113 PERSONAL FINANCE (38 Contact Hours--2 Credits) This course provides a survey of the major economic decisions facing the typical American household and examines the influence of social and economic change on individual financial planning. Students acquire the knowledge and develop the necessary analytical skills to make informed choices related to topics such as managing finances and budgeting, banking and saving, earning and reporting income, buying goods and services, using credit, and protecting against risk. This course puts emphasis on goal setting, lifelong learning, and active decision making.

5 Things I learned in Class

  • I learned the differences between gross pay and net pay and how to calculate each of them
  • I learned how to make a budget for my life based on net pay
  • I learned the basic fundimentals of banking and how loans work
  • I learned about credit cards and how to calculate how much they will cost me
  • I learned how student loans and mortgages are calculated
